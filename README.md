# Rebounder Pro — Your Personal Shooting Partner

**Get more shots up, in less time, and actually see yourself improve.**

Rebounder Pro is an automatic basketball shooting trainer. It sends the ball
back to you, spot after spot around the court, and keeps score while you shoot —
so you can run game-like reps on your own, stay in rhythm, and track exactly how
much you're getting better.

No rebounder needed. No one to pass to you. Just you, the ball, and the work.

## Why players love it

- **Way more reps.** Spend your time shooting, not chasing the ball.
- **Game-like movement.** The ball comes to different spots so you're shooting on
  the move, just like in a game.
- **Instant feedback.** See your makes, misses, and shooting percentage live.
- **Real progress tracking.** Every session is saved so you can look back and see
  how much you've improved — spot by spot.
- **Train solo.** No partner, no rebounder, no excuses.
- **Made for everyone.** Kids, high-schoolers, college players, and pros can all
  set it to the right challenge level.

## How it works, in plain terms

You set up a workout on the touchscreen, step onto the court, and start shooting.
The machine feeds you the ball at the spots you chose. You shoot, it feeds you
again. Behind the scenes it's keeping track of where you're hot and where you
need work — and it saves all of it so you can watch yourself grow over time.

The playing area is organized into **15 shooting spots** across the half court —
five positions from side to side, at three distances from the basket (think
close range, mid-range, and long range). You decide which spots to work on.

## Getting started in 4 steps

1. **Power on.** Turn on the machine and the touchscreen. Give it a few moments
   to wake up and get ready.
2. **Pick your profile.** Choose your name from the list so your session gets
   saved to *you*. New here? Create a profile in a few taps.
3. **Choose your workout.** Pick a training mode and a difficulty — or load one
   of your saved custom workouts.
4. **Grab a ball and go.** Tap start, get to your spot, and start shooting.

That's it. Everything else is on the screen.

## The four ways to train

Pick the format that matches what you want to work on today.

| Mode | What it means | Great for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Attempts** | Take a set number of shots at each spot, then move on. | Getting up a lot of reps and building a rhythm. |
| **Timed** | Shoot at each spot for a set amount of time, then move on. | Conditioning and shooting under a clock. |
| **Makes** | Keep shooting at a spot until you make a set number. | Finishing drills and building confidence. |
| **Streak** | Chase a run of makes in a row — a miss resets it. | Focus, pressure, and clutch shooting. |

For every mode you also choose **which spots** on the floor you want to work on,
and how the machine moves between them (in order, or mixed up).

## Easy, Hard, or your own Custom workout

- **Easy** — friendlier pacing and simpler patterns. Perfect for warming up,
  younger players, or grooving your form.
- **Hard** — faster pace, more shots, and mixed-up spots. Built to push you.
- **Custom** — you're the coach. Choose everything: the mode, the number, the
  spots, the order, and the timing. Save it and run it again any time.

## Building a custom workout

The Custom screen lets you dial in a session exactly how you want it:

1. **Pick your mode** — Attempts, Timed, Makes, or Streak.
2. **Set the number** — how many shots, how many seconds, or how many makes.
3. **Choose your spots** — tap the spots on the court you want to shoot from. Tap
   in the order you want to visit them, or let the machine choose the order.
4. **Set the pace** — how the machine moves through your spots:
   - **Auto / Random / Reverse** options change whether spots come in order,
     shuffled, or backwards — so you're never just going through the motions.
   - **Delay between passes** — how long you get between each ball.
5. **Start Delay (0–10 seconds)** — a countdown after you press start, so you have
   time to put the tablet down and get into position before the first ball. Set it
   to zero to start right away. When the countdown is running, a big number fills
   the screen and your settings are locked in.
6. **Save it** — give it a name and it's ready to load next time.

> Tip: Build a few favorites — a warm-up, a shooting workout, and a "pressure"
> streak drill — and just load the right one each day.

## During your session

While you shoot, the court screen keeps you informed at a glance:

- **Your live shooting percentage on every spot**, shown right on the floor. Spots
  fill in with color as you go, so you can instantly see where you're hot and
  where you're cold.
- **The active spot is highlighted** so you always know where the next ball is
  going.
- **Pause any time** — step off, grab water, then pick right back up. Your timer
  and score wait for you.
- **Score view** gives you the big picture: makes, attempts, percentage, and time.

When you're done, your whole session is saved to your profile automatically.

## Seeing your progress

This is where the work pays off. The progress and history screens are built to
answer one question: **"Am I getting better?"**

- **Overall summary** — your shooting across all your sessions at a glance.
- **Trend badges** — quick up/down indicators that show whether you're improving.
- **Session history** — every workout you've done, with the ability to see your
  best sessions and how each one compares to the last.
- **Improvement over time** — easy-to-read graphs that show your shooting
  percentage climbing session after session.
- **Your best spots and your work-ons** — see which areas of the floor are your
  strengths and which deserve more reps.

Come back after a few weeks and the difference is right there in front of you.

## Coach & player tips

- **Warm up first.** Start Easy and close to the basket, then work your way out.
- **Chase quality, not just quantity.** Use *Makes* or *Streak* mode when you want
  to lock in on form and finishing.
- **Simulate the game.** Turn on random spot order so you're always adjusting,
  just like in a real game.
- **Use the start delay.** Give yourself a couple of seconds to set your feet
  before the first ball.
- **Track one thing at a time.** Pick a weak spot, work it for a couple of weeks,
  and watch the percentage climb in your history.
- **Make it a habit.** Short, focused sessions several times a week beat one long
  session once in a while.

## Staying safe

Please read this section before your first session — it keeps everyone safe and
the machine happy.

- **Keep the launch area clear.** Make sure teammates, kids, and pets are out of
  the ball's path and away from the machine while it's running.
- **Don't reach into the machine** while it's on. If you need to clear a ball,
  pause or power down first.
- **Use the right ball.** Use a properly inflated basketball of the recommended
  size. A flat or oversized ball can cause jams.
- **Give it room.** Set up with enough space around the machine so it can move
  and feed freely.
- **Supervise younger players.** Kids should always use it with an adult present.
- **Built-in safety stop.** If something gets in the machine's way, it stops on
  its own to protect you and itself. If that happens, clear the area, check for
  anything stuck, and restart.

---

## Looking after your machine

- Keep it **dry** and store it indoors, away from rain and direct sun.
- **Wipe it down** now and then with a soft, dry cloth. Keep dust and grit off it.
- Keep the **touchscreen clean** with a soft cloth — no harsh cleaners.
- Before each session, do a quick look-over to make sure nothing is loose or in
  the way.
- If you're moving it, do so gently and let it settle before powering on.

## Troubleshooting

**The screen and machine aren't connected yet (indicator isn't green).**
Give it a little time — they reconnect automatically. Make sure both the machine
and the touchscreen are powered on and close to each other. If it's taking a
while, it will keep trying on its own.

**The ball isn't coming out, or comes out weak.**
Check that you're using a properly inflated ball of the right size, and that
nothing is blocking the feed area. Pause, clear anything in the way, and resume.

**The machine stopped by itself mid-session.**
That's the safety stop doing its job. Clear the area, make sure nothing is stuck,
then start a new session.

**My score didn't look right.**
Make sure you're shooting at the highlighted spot and using the same ball each
time. Very fast or unusual shots can occasionally be missed by the counter.

**A ball got stuck.**
Power down or pause first, then gently remove it. Never reach in while it's
running.

**Still stuck?** Turn everything off, wait a moment, and power back on — a fresh
start solves most hiccups. If a problem keeps happening, reach out to support.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need anyone to help me train?**
No. That's the whole point — it feeds you the ball so you can train solo.

**Can more than one person use it?**
Yes. Each player picks their own profile, so everyone's scores and progress stay
separate.

**Can I make my own drills?**
Absolutely. Build custom workouts, save them, and load them any time.

**Does it remember my results?**
Every session is saved to your profile so you can track your improvement over
time.

**What level is this for?**
Everyone — from first-timers to advanced players. Just choose Easy, Hard, or a
Custom workout that fits you.

**How many spots can I work on?**
There are 15 spots across the half court. Use one, a few, or all of them.
